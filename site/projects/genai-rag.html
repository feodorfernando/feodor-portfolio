🤖 GenAI Catalog Assistant (RAG)

Ask a data lakehouse questions in plain English — "which tables contain PII and who owns them?" — and get answers grounded in catalog metadata, with citations to the exact tables used.

What it is

A retrieval-augmented assistant over a lakehouse catalog (schemas, lineage, ownership, PII flags, freshness). Catalog entries are embedded locally; a question retrieves the most relevant tables, and Claude answers only from that retrieved context — citing table names and refusing to invent tables or columns. It's a clean, guard-railed pattern for turning trapped catalog knowledge into a conversational interface.

Design & architecture

How I built it

  • Local embeddings (all-MiniLM-L6-v2) over rendered catalog documents — no external embedding vendor, normalized vectors for cosine via dot product.
  • Retrieve top-k relevant tables for the question and stuff their metadata into a <context> block.
  • Strict system prompt: answer only from context, cite table names, say "not in catalog" rather than hallucinate.
  • Model-swappable: Sonnet by default; Opus for hardest reasoning, Haiku to cut cost/latency.
